    Section 8 Solution Technique 6: Non-homogeneous Second Order ODEs

    This note gives a useful technique for solving second-order constant-coefficient ODEs that are not homogeneous. That is, we wish to solve
    \begin{equation} ax'' + bx' + cx = F(t),\tag{8.1} \end{equation}
    where the function \(F(t)\) is nonzero. Often, \(F(t)\) corresponds to some external force (not from the spring or damper).

    Subsection The Method of Undetermined Coefficients

    Theorem 8.1. Form of Solutions to Second-Order Linear ODEs.

    The general solution to the ODE (8.1) is
    \begin{equation*} x(t) = x_h(t) + x_p(t) \end{equation*}
    where \(x_h(t)\) is the general solution to the homogeneous first-order ODE
    \begin{equation} ax'' + bx' + cx = 0\tag{8.2} \end{equation}
    and \(x_p(t)\) is any particular solution to (8.1) you may be able to find.

    Proof.

    Let \(x(t)\) be an arbitrary solution to (8.1). Consider the difference \(x(t) - x_p(t)\text{.}\) Observe that this is a solution to (8.2). This means \(x(t)\) has the form indicated by the theorem.
